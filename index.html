About

From fixing phones to fixing spreadsheets

I started out as a mobile technician, learning to diagnose problems methodically, a habit that turned out to translate directly into data work. From there I moved into HR, where I got comfortable with people, process, and the kind of ambiguity that spreadsheets alone can't solve.

Somewhere along the way I found myself gravitating toward the numbers side of every role: building trackers, cleaning data, and asking what a number actually means before I knew "data analyst" was a job title I could pursue.

Today I'm building my skills in SQL, Python, and data visualization, with a focus on marketing and customer analytics. I like problems where the output isn't just a chart, but a decision.

Customer Segmentation with RFM Scoring

Business problem

UrbanStyle needed to know which customers to prioritize for retention, and which campaign to send to whom, instead of treating every customer the same.

What I did

Built an RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) scoring model in pandas, segmenting the full customer base into actionable groups.

Result

Identified VIP customers, customers at risk of churning, and matched each segment to a specific campaign recommendation.

Automated Sales Data Pipeline

Business problem

Refreshing the sales analysis by hand every time new data landed in Supabase was slow and error-prone.

What I did

Built a Python ETL pipeline: extract from the Supabase API with pagination, transform and score with pandas, and export to CSV and an interactive Plotly dashboard.

Result

A repeatable, one-command refresh that replaced hours of manual copy-pasting and cut down on human error.

Sales Data Cleaning & Audit

Business problem

Result

A clean, trustworthy dataset that the rest of the programme's analysis, including the RFM model, could be built on.
